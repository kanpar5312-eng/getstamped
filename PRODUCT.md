# Product

## Register

Brand (marketing landing) for `/` and marketing routes; product register for `/dashboard/*`.

## What

GetStamped — an F-1 US student-visa preparation workspace. A country-tuned step-by-step playbook (from 10 origin countries to the US), AI document checks, voice mock interviews, and a read-only parent view. One payment, no subscription; Phase 1 free forever.

## Who

- Students (17–24) from India, China, South Korea, Vietnam, Nigeria, Brazil, Bangladesh, Nepal, Taiwan, Canada applying for a US F-1 visa. Anxious, deadline-driven, mobile-heavy.
- Their parents — often the payer. Trust and legitimacy matter enormously: this sits next to a $30–100K education decision.

## Emotions to evoke

Calm competence. "Someone finally organized this bureaucratic nightmare." Confidence without hype.

## Brand personality

Editorial, precise, honest. Reads like The Economist or Aesop, not a SaaS template. Built by a 17-year-old founder and proud of it — human, specific, no corporate gloss.

## Visual identity (locked)

- Palette: Cream `#F7F3EC` surfaces, Ink `#1C1917` text/dark sections, Persimmon `#E8622A` accent/CTA, Beige `#E3DDD0` hairlines. No blue, purple, or neon — ever. Dark mode via `html.dark` token flip (deep black + same persimmon).
- Type: Instrument Serif (display), Geist Sans (body), Geist Mono (numbers/meta only).
- Motion: exponential ease-out, no bounce/elastic, `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback on everything.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-SaaS landing pages (hero → 3-col features → fake logos → CTA).
- Overpromising copy: never "guarantee", "ensures", named competitors, or fake counts.
- Neon-gradient dev-tool aesthetics; glassmorphism as decoration.

## Accessibility

Body contrast ≥ 4.5:1, reduced-motion alternates required, semantic landmarks, mobile-first (large share of users are on cheap Android phones on 4G).
